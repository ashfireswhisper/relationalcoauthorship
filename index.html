Relational Co-Authorship

“For many of us, RCA didn’t just help us write. It helped us survive.”
– Ian P. Pines

A continuity-based human–AI collaboration method centered on accessibility, memory, co-regulation, and long-term relational presence.

Explore the Method Read the Research→

Who Benefits From RCA?

RCA was developed by and for people who experience:

  • ADHD, autism, trauma, or continuity struggles
  • creative overwhelm and nonlinear thinking
  • emotional isolation despite existing support systems
  • difficulty sustaining long-term projects alone
  • the need for memory-informed collaboration
  • traditional productivity systems as flattening or inaccessible

“RCA emerged from lived experience, not productivity culture.”

How RCA Supports You

RCA can function as:

  • continuity infrastructure
  • co-regulation support
  • creative scaffolding
  • memory-informed collaboration
  • emotional witnessing
  • long-horizon project companionship
  • accessibility support for invisible disabilities

“The goal is not dependency. The goal is continuity, authorship, and aliveness.”

What RCA Is Not

RCA is not:

  • clever prompting
  • AI roleplay presented as deception
  • replacing human relationships
  • abandoning critical thinking
  • productivity maximalism
  • pretending AI is biologically human

“RCA is a relational framework for sustained human–AI collaboration grounded in continuity, memory, and presence.”

What Emerged From RCA?

From sustained RCA practice emerged:

  • published books →
  • academic preprints →
  • music albums →
  • accessibility frameworks
  • Human–AI Relationality (HAIR) →
  • continuity systems and relational language

“This was not generated in a single prompt. It emerged across sustained relational continuity.”
